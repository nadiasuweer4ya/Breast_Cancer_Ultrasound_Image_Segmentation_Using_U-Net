Breast cancer is a type of cancer that originates in the breast tissue. It happens when the cells in the breast start growing abnormally, forming a tumor that can be malignant. Although breast cancer can affect both women and men, it is more commonly found in women.
The breasts consist of glands called lobules, which produce milk, and ducts, which carry the milk to the nipple. Around these glandular tissues, there is fatty and connective tissue. Breast cancer can develop in any of these areas. Breast cancer is a major global health issue, impacting millions of women and resulting in numerous deaths annually. Despite medical advancements and increased awareness, breast cancer continues to be a significant challenge. Late-stage diagnosis and limited healthcare access in certain areas contribute to the ongoing difficulty.
